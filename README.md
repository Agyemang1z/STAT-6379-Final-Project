This study employs stochastic modeling to explore epidemic dynamics through a Susceptible-Infectious-Recovered (SIR) compartment model, utilizing both Poisson and Binomial distributions to simulate infectious disease spread. We focus on the unpredictable nature of disease transmission and recovery, examining key parameters like the basic reproduction number and conducting a sensitivity analysis to assess how parameter changes affect disease dynamics. Additionally, the study explores the effectiveness of public health interventions such as vaccination and social distancing. Our findings highlight the value of stochastic models in understanding and managing disease spread, particularly under conditions of uncertainty, and underscore the critical role of timely public health interventions in controlling outbreaks. We wish to extend the analysis of this study to a communicable disease in the future.
